DHW_Energy_Tapping

Path: CARNOT/Load/Hot_Water_Tapping

Purpose:
Create a hot water consumption with an energy profile.

Description:
The domestic hot water energy tapping produces a tapping profile and a set point temperature according to the the specified temperatures, times, massflows and energies.
At a each given start time a tapping is created which fullfills the equation:

     tapping duration = energy_of_tap / (massflow_coefficient * nominal_massflow * heat_capacity * delta_temperature)

The output mdot is set to :

     mdot = massflow_coefficient * nominal_massflow

The set point temperature Tset is :

     Tset = Tc + delta_temperature

Several domestic hot water comfort evaluation methods define the consumption as an energy profile and a temperature difference to achieve. Examples are the European Tapping Cycles specified by the European Commission COMMISSION REGULATION (EU) No 814/2013 of 2 August 2013.

These tapping cylces are used by EN13203-1. The EN targets gas fired hot water preparation units with a maximum volume of 300 l and a maximum power of 70 kW. It introduces a method for the assessment of the energy consumption of the hot water preparation unit as well as a method for the assessment of hot water comfort [QAIST 2012]. The EN 16147 is the similar standard for electric driven heat pumps.

Input:

Tc : cold water temperature in °C

Output:

mdot : massflow of the tapping in kg/s
Tset : hot water set point temperature in °C
