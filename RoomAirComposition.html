RoomAirComposition
Path: CARNOT/Basic/Thermal_Models

Purpose
Model for the concentration of CO2 and VOC in the room air. Additionally the humidity in the room is damped by a transfer function (NO full humidity model).

Description

Concentration of gases (CO2 and VOC) are calculated by the differential equations used to model mixing or dilution problems.
If y is the quantity of a gas in a room, then its rate of change with respect to time t will be given by

    dy/dt = flow in − flow out

With
   flow = concentration × velocity
and  
   concentration = quantity  volume 

We get

   dy/dt = y_coming_in/volume × velocity_in − y_going out/volume × velocity out

For constant boundary conditions the equation can be solved analytically. As boundary conditions may vary, the model leaves this task to Simulink.

Remark: The calculation can be disabled by the check-box "Calculate air composition".

Inputs

  • THBvent - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of all air flows entering the room (air gaps, window ventilation, ventilation system and external air supply)
  • Troom - room temperature in °C  
  • RDB - Resident Data Bus, use Resident_Data or Redident_Task44 to create the bus
  • THBsupplyAir - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of an external ventilation system. The mass flow in this THB sets the mass flow in THBexhaustAir.

Outputs

  • THBvent_out - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the air leaging the room, the massflow is the total massflow of all air exchange as given in THBvent
  • xH2O - absolute humidity of the room air in kg of water per kg of dry air
  • CO2 - concentration of CO2 in ppm
  • VOC - concentration of VOC in ppm
  • THBexhaustyAir - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the exhaust air to the external ventilation system. The mass flow in this THB is set by the mass flow in THBexhaustAir. All air properties are taken from the room air (temperature, humidity, concentration).
