Function Call

Re = reynolds(t, v, p, ft, fm, d)

Inputs

(scalar or vector)
t    :   temperature in °C
v    : velocity of the fluid flow in m/s
p    :   pressure in Pa
ft   :   FluidEnum class or fluid ID number
fm   :   fluid_mix
d    :   characteristic dimension [m]

Outputs

Re   : Reynolds number [-]

Description

The function caclulates the Reynolds number of a fluid flow. The
function kinematic_viscosity of carlib is used.
 Re = v * d / nue
 with:  nue - kinematic viscosity in m²/s

Examples

1) Reynolds number of water flow in a pipe (0.2 m diameter)
 with a velocity of 0.9 m/s at 20 °C and 1000 hPa
 Re = reynolds(20, 0.9, 1e5, 1, 0, 0.2)
2) Reynolds number of water flow in a pipe (0.2 m diameter)
 with a velocity of 0.9 m/s at 20 °C and 1000 hPa, using FluidEnum
 Re = reynolds(20, 0.9, 1e5, FluidEnum.WATER, 0, 0.2)
3) Reynolds number of water flow in a pipe (0.2 m diameter)
 with a velocity of 0.9 m/s at 20 °C, 40 °C, 60 °C and 1000 hPa, using FluidEnum
 Re = reynolds([20, 40, 60], 0.9, 1e5, FluidEnum.WATER, 0, 0.2)
4) Reynolds number of water flow in a pipe (0.2 m diameter)
 with a velocity of 0.9 m/s, 1.1 m/s, 1.3 m/s at 20 °C and 1000 hPa, using FluidEnum
 Re = reynolds(20, 0.9:0.2:1.3, 1e5, FluidEnum.WATER, 0, 0.2)

References and Literature

See also FluidEnum, Carlib, prandtl, grashof, velocity, kinematic_viscosity
