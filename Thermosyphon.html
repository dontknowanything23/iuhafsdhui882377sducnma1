Thermosyphon

Path: CARNOT/Hydraulics


Purpose:
Characteristic of a system that works with thermosyphon effect to pump the fluid.

Description:
Characteristic of system that works with the thermosyphon effect, that means the pump effect is achieved by differences in density. These differences in density exist because of the temperature differences of the fluid at different positions in the cycle. The physical effect in which the hotter fluid rises to the top and the colder one sinks to the bottom is used to build up a natural pumping cycle that is maintained by the temperature and accordingly the density differences of the fluid. The input of the thermosyphon block is just the THV. The temperature is adapted according to the heat capacity of the block and the pressure is calculated as in any other pump block.
As the thermosyphon cycle has no pump, it has to be a closed cycle, and you have to guarantee in every case that the heights of the system are equal. In the model a check of the height is implemented. The sum of the height in the Thermo-Hydraulic Bus has to be zero.

Input:

THBin : Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the incomming flow

Output:

THB : Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the leaving flow
