Collector 1xN
Path: CARNOT/Source/Solar_Thermal

 

 

 

Purpose
Collector model, that calculates the THB output.

Description
The collector model is a one-dimensional multinode model for thermal collectors. The model is based on parameters obtained from measurement, i.e. collector-test results. The collector is divided in "Node" nodes. The flow is equally distributed among the risers.

As the 1xN approach is a better fit to the real temperature increas along the flow in a collector, the model is suitable for low flow systems and systems with a varying (low) massflow like thermosyphon systems.

The energy balance for every node is a differential equation, that is calculated in the S-function unicol.

ccoll * dT/dt = qsolar + mdot*c/A * (Tin - T)

      + U1*(Tamb - T) - U2*(Tamb - T)2

      + Uwind*vwind*(Tamb - T) + Usky*(Tsky - T)

The collector calculates friction losses in the massflow. This may be specified in the subsystem Pressure_Drop_Collector of the collector. Static pressure is calculated if necessary, i.e. for thermosyphon systems.
See chapter 2.4.1 Pressure Drop and Massflow Calculations of the manual 2_Basic_Concepts.

Inputs:

  • WDB - Weather Data Bus
  • collector position defined by the Fixed_Surface block
  • THB - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the collector inlet

Outputs:

  • THB - Thermo-Hydraulic Bus of the collector outlet
  • Cdat - Collector Data Bus for logging and display, see create_Cdat

Validation

  • The model was checked with results of earlier TRNSYS simulations of the Solar Campus Jülich, where the Isakson collector model (type 101) was used. The discrepancy in power was smaller than 2 %.
  • A comparison of the model with test data of a flat plate collector has been done and shows low difference. The model Collector_flat_plate in Chapter 2.1.2 of the valudation report is now the Collector_1xN model.
